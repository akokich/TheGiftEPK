Cast


David Drummond - Lead Actor


David is a blue-collar artist living in Seattle. He’s made his living as a performer for nearly thirty years, in all media. His first love is working on stage. He’s performed at many theaters in Seattle and Los Angeles, most recently playing the title role in Shakespeare’s Coriolanus at Seattle Shakespeare Company. On TV and in film, he has specialized in playing security guards and jealous boyfriends, most recently branching out as a Nazi torturer in The Man in the High Castle. He is also an award-winning audiobook narrator, with over 150 books recorded in his home studio.

Riley Neldam - Lead Actor


Riley is a native of Seattle and has performed on may of that city’s professional stages over the past five years, including; Seattle Shakespeare Company, 5th Avenue Theatre, Taproot Theatre, Book-It-Repetory Theatre, and Galagan Theatre, as well as regional tours throughout the pacific northwest with both SCC/Wooden O and Freehold Studio’s Engaged Theatre Tour. Notable TV appearances include recurring and co-starring roles on the PBS original program Biz Kid$, as well as the SyFy Network original series Z Nation filmed in Seattle and Spokane respectively. Riley recently relocated to Los Angeles but will always consider Seattle to be home.

Crew


Mardig Sheridan - Director


Mardig’s creative journey began at age twelve. He loved movies and music. Although his Air Force Sargent dad couldn’t afford a film camera, he bought him a three-dollar guitar. Mardig would lie in bed at night listening to his transistor radio and, strumming away, dreaming of becoming a rock star. At age twenty he became almost famous as part of popular northwest rock band The Bards. After their one-hit wonder, they recorded an album with legendary producers Curt Boetcher and Keith Olsen, then promptly broke up. Lacking any marketable skills, Mardig opened an advertising agency and soon realized commercial film directors were having all the fun. He sold the agency and started directing. He co-wrote a terrific biopic of seminal 50’s rocker Gene Vincent and trudged around Hollywood trying to get it made (he hasn’t given up on that one). The Gift is his first dramatic film.

Clark Kokich - Writer & Executive Producer


Clark lives in Seattle, where he wasted his life in the world of advertising, most recently as C.E.O. of Razorfish, a global leader in digital marketing. In 2011 Clark wrote and produced Do or Die, the world’s first business book delivered as an interactive app for the iPad. He left the agency business two years ago to pursue a career in film. His first short, 20/20 was the world’s first narrative film shot with Google Glass. It was screened at eleven festivals worldwide in 2014 and managed to somehow generate over 50,000 YouTube views. He’s written two feature screenplays. One truly sucked and the other was merely bad. He’s working on his third, which he hopes to be fair to middlin’ (it’s a journey). Expect him to write something filmable some time around mid-2016.

Chris Oliver - Producer


Chris is an Emmy Award winning producer and member of the Producer’s Guild of America and the National Academy of Television Arts & Sciences. His career has included producing, directing, writing, and production of feature films, short films, commercials, and television shows. He is the president and founder of Wishing Star Pictures. His most recent credits include multi-award winning short films, a PBS Nova special, and the international television show The Artist Toolbox. He has worked with stars such as Tom Skerritt, Eric Roberts, Elizabeth Rohm, John Legend and Jason Alexander. He currently has numerous film projects in various stages of development in Seattle and Los Angeles.

Mike Boydstun - Director of Photography


Mike Boydstun is a two-time Grammy nominated director, three time Daytime Emmy nominated cinematographer, winner of TELE, IRIS, ANNIE, and international MONITOR awards, plus two RIAA Multi-Platinum releases. He has worked with actors the likes of Lance Henriksen, Tom Skerritt, Edward Furlong, Gary Busey, Lee Majors, and Margo Kidder, plus has collaborated with world-class musicians Jan Hammer, Thomas Dolby, Carl Perkins, Tangerine Dream, and Queensryche. Mike’s feature film credits include Matt’s Chance, The Penitent Man, and That Night.

Vince Werner - Editor


Vince has been doing editorial work in the last few years after having spent over two decades in sound design and mixing. He first worked with writer/producer Clark Kokich directing and editing the short 20/20, and has edited many corporate films and ads. His sound design and mixing credits include Max Rules, Safety Not Guaranteed, Penitent Man, and many more. Vince co-founded Seattle post house Clatter&Din in 1994 following ten years mixing at Bad Animals. He is a graduate of The Evergreen State College and lives with his wife Mary in a cabin in the woods.

Lisa Hammond - Production Designer


Lisa received her BFA in Photography from the University of California at Santa Cruz. After years in advertising, she shifted her focus to independent film. Her credits include feature films Safety Not Guaranteed, The Off Hours, and The Details, all Official Selections at the Sundance Film Festival, as well as Z-Nation on the SyFy Channel. Her commercial clients include: Target, BECU, Swiss Army, Lands End, Wyeth Pharmaceutical, Amazon, and Microsoft. Lisa editorial work has been seen in Vogue, Wired, Sunset, Flaunt, Forbes, TV Guide and Sports Illustrated.
